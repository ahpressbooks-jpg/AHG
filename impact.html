How We Measure

What we count, and why it matters.

AHG measures impact across three dimensions: policy outcomes, organizational capacity, and community conditions. We do not count deliverables as impact. A report that sits unread is not an outcome. A policy that passes but goes unenforced is not a win.

Our standard for impact is simple: did conditions change for the people the work was designed to benefit? If the answer is yes — and we can demonstrate it with evidence — then we earned the win. If the answer is no, we want to know why, and we take that question seriously.

This page will document the wins as they come. AHG was built for longevity. The record will grow.

Policy Outcomes

Legislation passed. Regulations changed. Funding streams secured. Policies repealed that should have been repealed years ago. These are the wins that create durable change at the systems level — and the ones AHG is most focused on building toward.

Organizational Capacity

Clients that are stronger, more focused, and more effective after working with AHG than before. A stronger organization means a stronger sector — and the mission outlasting any single engagement or grant cycle.

Community Conditions

The question beneath all the work: are the people the institutions are supposed to serve actually better off? This is the measure that keeps everything else honest — and the one we hold ourselves and our clients to.

Focus Areas

Where the work is concentrated.

AHG's policy and advocacy work is concentrated in five areas where the gap between what institutions promise and what they deliver is largest — and where strategic intervention has the most leverage.

Foster Care System Reform

Texas has one of the largest foster care populations in the nation and one of the most underfunded systems. AHG's long-term mission is anchored in changing how the state treats its most vulnerable young people — from policy to practice to physical infrastructure.

Education Equity & Access

From early childhood through post-secondary completion, AHG works on the policy and institutional conditions that determine whether a student's zip code or family circumstance determines their educational outcome. They shouldn't. We work to ensure they don't.

Workforce & Economic Mobility

Economic opportunity should not be distributed by birth. AHG works with workforce systems, employers, and community organizations to build pathways into careers with real wages, real advancement, and real futures for communities that have been systematically excluded from both.

Community Infrastructure

Strong communities require strong institutions — schools, nonprofits, civic organizations, and government agencies that are competent, accountable, and trusted. AHG builds organizational capacity because institutional strength is itself a form of community infrastructure.
