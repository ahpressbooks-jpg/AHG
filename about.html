About AHG

This work is personal.
It always has been.

Almost Human Group was not founded to fill a market gap. It was founded because the people who need the most help are still waiting — and someone had to stop waiting with them.

Founder & Mission

Built from conviction, not convenience.

AHG was founded on the belief that strategy, when it's serious, changes lives — and that organizations doing difficult work deserve the same quality of counsel as the institutions that oppose them.

Almost Human Group was born at the intersection of lived experience and professional expertise. Our founder spent years working inside and alongside the systems that shape how communities access education, economic opportunity, and political power — and saw, repeatedly, the gap between what institutions promised and what they actually delivered.

The firm's name is not an accident. "Almost human" captures what too many institutions are: close to the people they serve, but not quite. Close to accountability, but not quite. Close to genuine transformation, but not quite. AHG exists to close that distance — for clients, for communities, and ultimately for a generation of young people in Texas who deserve more than a system designed to manage them rather than develop them.

We operate from Dallas-Fort Worth because this region is where the work is urgent, where the population is growing, and where the political and philanthropic landscape is ripe for the kind of institution-building that creates lasting change. Texas is not a detour — it is the destination.

Our engagements are selective by design. We do not take on more than we can do with excellence. We do not issue generic recommendations. And we do not stay silent when a client's plan needs to be stronger. We are partners in the fullest sense — which means we bring honesty, not just agreement.

AHG's mission is to strengthen the organizations, campaigns, and institutions that are working to build a more just, more functional, and more humane Texas — one policy, one program, one partnership at a time.

Our Values

What guides every engagement.

Legacy Over Transaction

Long-Term Vision

The Almost Human Charter Community.

Everything AHG does — every policy engagement, every communications strategy, every program design — is oriented toward a single long-term goal: the creation of the Almost Human Charter Community, a residential charter school in Dallas-Fort Worth built exclusively for youth in and aging out of the foster care system.

Texas has more than 29,000 children in foster care on any given day. The overwhelming majority of them are in the DFW metro area and surrounding counties. They face compounding disadvantages: educational disruption, housing instability, legal system involvement, and a near-total absence of the kind of relational permanency that other young people take for granted.

AHCC will not be a last resort. It will be a school of excellence — with high academic expectations, wraparound support, trauma-informed culture, and a post-secondary pipeline that actually delivers. It will be the institution we build because no one else is building it, and the need cannot wait.

AHG's consulting practice funds the legal, policy, and organizational groundwork for AHCC. When you engage AHG, you are investing in a firm building toward something larger than any single client relationship.
