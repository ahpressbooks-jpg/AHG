BRIDGE Ages 14 - 18

Breaking Rules In Decisions that Govern Education

"Students don't just attend school. They should help design it."

The Problem

Decisions Without Input

School boards make decisions that define the daily lives of students: discipline policies, curriculum requirements, mental health resources, dress codes, scheduling. Every one of those decisions is made by adults who haven't been in a classroom in decades.

Student government exists, but it plans dances. It doesn't shape policy. When students do speak at a school board meeting, they get three minutes and a polite nod. That isn't input. That's theater.

What BRIDGE Is

A Seat at the Table

BRIDGE is AHG's school-based advocacy program. We partner with high schools across DFW to create a formal channel between students and the people who make decisions about their education.

Students don't just learn about advocacy in the abstract. They identify a real problem in their school, research it, build a policy brief, and present it to their school board with data, solutions, and a demand for a response.

This is not a suggestion box. This is a seat at the table.

Program Structure

Six Phases. One Outcome.

Every BRIDGE cohort moves through a structured sequence designed to turn frustration into formal action.

Phase 01

Issue Identification

Students identify the problems that affect them most inside their school. Not hypothetical issues. Real ones. The ones they walk through every day and nobody asks them about.

Phase 02

Research & Data Collection

Cohort members learn how to gather evidence: surveys, interviews, public data requests, and observation. They build the evidentiary foundation that turns a complaint into a case.

Phase 03

Policy Brief Writing

Students draft a formal policy brief that includes the problem, evidence, proposed solution, and implementation path. The same format used by professional policy analysts.

Phase 04

Presentation Development

Public speaking training, slide design, anticipating objections, and practicing under pressure. When they walk into that room, they will be the most prepared people in it.

Phase 05

School Board Presentation

The culmination. Students present their policy brief directly to their school board. Not a three-minute comment. A formal presentation with data, solutions, and a request for action.

Phase 06

Network & Alumni

Graduates join the BRIDGE alumni network, gain access to AHG's broader programming, and become mentors for the next cohort. The pipeline doesn't end. It accelerates.
