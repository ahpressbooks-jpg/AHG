SEAT Ages 16 - 24

Students Earning A Table

"The seat was always yours. We just help you pull it out."

The Problem

The Room Was Never Empty

Look at any city council, school board, planning commission, or legislative body. The people making decisions about the future are almost never young. They are rarely from the communities most affected by those decisions. And they were almost never asked to explain why they believe they should be making those decisions alone.

The solution isn't waiting for the next generation to age into power. The solution is putting young people in the room now, with the preparation to be taken seriously and the authority to make change.

What SEAT Is

Real Authority. Real Preparation.

SEAT trains the next generation of civic leaders to run for office, serve on boards, and occupy decision-making spaces. Not as tokens. Not as youth representatives who get a pat on the head and no vote. As actual decision-makers with real authority.

We teach the mechanics of how civic power actually works, because most people who hold it were never formally taught either. They just grew up in families and networks where they absorbed it. SEAT closes that gap with structure.

Every SEAT graduate leaves with a personal civic action plan and the skills to execute it.

Program Structure

Eight Weeks. One Mission.

SEAT runs as an eight-week intensive cohort. Each week builds on the last. Every session is designed to move participants from understanding to action.

Week 01 - 02

Civic Mechanics

How government actually works at the local, state, and federal level. Not the textbook version. The real version. Who holds power, how decisions get made, and where the leverage points are.

Week 03 - 04

Public Speaking & Communication

Participants learn to speak with authority and clarity in public settings. Council chambers, boardrooms, press conferences, community forums. Every environment where civic leaders must perform under pressure.

Week 05

Parliamentary Procedure

Robert's Rules. Motions, amendments, points of order. The procedural framework that governs every formal decision-making body. Most adults in those rooms don't fully understand it. SEAT graduates will.

Week 06 - 07

Campaign Strategy

How to run for office, build a coalition, fundraise, and communicate a platform. Whether they run next year or in ten years, graduates will know the mechanics of competitive civic engagement.

Week 08

Civic Action Plan

Every graduate develops and presents a personal civic action plan: the specific office, board, or commission they intend to pursue, the timeline, and the steps to get there. This is not aspirational. This is operational.

Claim Your Seat

The Table Is Waiting.

SEAT is open to young people ages 16-24 in DFW ready to lead, not someday, but now.
