Future Leaders On Real Reform

"Legislation is written by people. You can be one of them."

The Problem

Fellowships for the Privileged

Policy fellowships exist. They place bright young people in government offices, think tanks, and legislative chambers. They build the pipeline for the next generation of policy leaders. And they are overwhelmingly filled by students from elite universities with parents who know how the system works.

The young person who aged out of foster care, who works two jobs, who has never seen the inside of a statehouse except on television — that person has never been the target audience for those programs.

FLOOR is for the people those programs never considered.

What FLOOR Is

Real Work. Real Relationships. Real Credentials.

FLOOR is AHG's flagship policy fellowship. It places competitively selected young adults inside government offices and legislative chambers. Not for tours. Not for observation. For real work.

Fellows draft policy language, attend committee hearings, build relationships with elected officials and senior staff, and produce a substantive policy deliverable that demonstrates their capacity to lead.

When they leave, they leave with credentials, a network, and a record of real contribution.

Selection

Commitment, Not Pedigree

FLOOR selects 8 to 12 fellows per cohort. The process is competitive, but the criteria are different from what you've seen elsewhere.

We don't select for GPA, university prestige, or family connections. We select for commitment, clarity of purpose, and the ability to do the work. If you have the drive and the discipline, FLOOR was built for you.

Fellowship Structure

Inside the Fellowship

FLOOR fellows don't observe from the sidelines. Every element of the fellowship is designed to build real capacity and real credentials.

Core Element

Placement in Government Office

Each fellow is placed in a government office or legislative chamber. They report to work. They attend meetings. They are part of the team. This is not a shadowing experience. It is a working placement.

Core Element

Structured Policy Curriculum

Fellows participate in a parallel policy curriculum covering legislative process, policy analysis, stakeholder mapping, and written communication. The classroom reinforces what they see in the field.

Core Element

Dual Mentorship

Every fellow is assigned an AHG mentor and an institutional supervisor from their placement office. Two perspectives. Two networks. Two people invested in their success.

Core Element

Weekly Cohort Sessions

Fellows meet weekly as a cohort to debrief, share challenges, workshop their policy deliverables, and build the relationships that will define their professional networks for years to come.

Culmination

Substantive Policy Deliverable

Every fellow produces one substantive policy deliverable: a brief, a legislative analysis, a program proposal, or a research paper. It is reviewed, refined, and presented. It goes on their record. It is real work product.
