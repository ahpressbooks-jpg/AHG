LAUNCH Ages 18 - 26

Leading A United Next Generation CHange

"Your zip code is your starting line. Not your ceiling."

The Problem

Information Failure

More than 50% of young adults aging out of foster care are unemployed. Not because they lack motivation. Because the system that was supposed to prepare them for adulthood never taught them the things adults need to know.

Nobody taught them about credit scores, salary negotiation, lease agreements, or how to build a professional network from scratch. These aren't character failures. They are information failures. The system didn't forget. It was never designed to remember.

LAUNCH exists to close that gap permanently.

What LAUNCH Is

The Tools the System Never Provided

LAUNCH serves young adults ages 18-26 who are building from nothing. Former foster youth, first-generation professionals, and anyone who aged out of institutional support without the tools to build a stable life.

This is not a motivational seminar. This is a structured, six-week program that gives participants the financial literacy, career navigation skills, professional development, and advocacy tools that families with resources pass down generationally.

We don't inspire people. We equip them.

Program Structure

Six Weeks. Real Tools.

Every LAUNCH cohort moves through five modules designed to build the foundation that lasts a lifetime.

Week 01

Financial Literacy

Credit scores, banking, budgeting, debt management, and savings strategies. Not abstract concepts. Real tools applied to real situations. Participants leave with a personal financial plan and the knowledge to maintain it.

Week 02 - 03

Career Navigation

Resume building, interview preparation, salary negotiation, and professional networking. How to find jobs, apply strategically, and navigate workplaces where no one taught you the unwritten rules.

Week 04

Professional Skills

Workplace communication, email etiquette, conflict resolution, and time management. The soft skills that determine whether someone survives or thrives in professional environments.

Week 05

Advocacy Tools

Self-advocacy in housing, employment, healthcare, and legal contexts. Knowing your rights, understanding systems, and learning how to push back when institutions fail you. Because the system will try again.

Week 06

Alumni Network

Graduates join the LAUNCH alumni network: ongoing mentorship, job referrals, professional development events, and access to AHG's broader programming. The support system doesn't end when the program does.

Build Your Future

Start Here.

LAUNCH is open to young adults ages 18-26 in DFW. No prerequisites. No fees. Just commitment.
