Who We Are

Not a Charity.
A Movement.

AHG is organized, structured, funded, and built to outlast the resistance. We use the same legal structure as the NAACP and the ACLU because the fight the next generation is in requires it. We can lobby. We can endorse candidates for office. We can name the officials who are failing the people we serve.

We serve every young person from a high school freshman to age 26 — the exact window where institutions either open doors or close them for good.

We go wherever the fight is.

The Origin

The Hallway.

AHG exists because too many people in this country are treated as almost human. Almost old enough to matter. Almost ready to have a voice. Almost worth designing a system around.

Our founder grew up inside those systems. He was moved, assessed, categorized, and processed. Not developed. Not asked what he needed. Not given a plan when being smart wasn't enough. The institution that was supposed to develop him decided he was fine and moved on.

He was not fine.

One door opened. He walked through it. On the other side was the understanding that his story didn't belong only to him — it belonged to every person still standing in the hallway, waiting, wondering if a door even exists for them.

That's who we build for.
