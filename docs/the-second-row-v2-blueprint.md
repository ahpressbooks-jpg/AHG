10. **Independence, structurally** — not claimed, *instrumented* (see The Tilt Meter, Part B).
11. **Mined the business plan again** — the Spin Room, the Ledger, certainty tags, Steelman Saturday, the education ladder, and The Room all get surface area in v2 (they're woven through Parts B and C).

# PART B · EVERYTHING I'D ADD

### Signature inventions (the never-heard-before list)

1. **GRAVITY — the named algorithm** (full spec in Part D). One sentence: *"Most feeds rank what gets clicked. We rank what carries weight."* Every story wears its Gravity score 0–100, and the score is tappable — the math opens.
2. **The Lens** — a draggable slider on any story card: slide left → see how the left is framing it *right now*; slide right → the right's framing; center → the wires. The Spin Room compressed into a single touchable interaction on every story. Nobody has this.
3. **Comments ranked by minds changed, not likes.** Readers can mark a comment "this moved me." The comment that changed the most minds sits on top — not the loudest one. The like button doesn't exist.
4. **Certainty tags on reader comments** — when you comment, you tag your own confidence: CERTAIN / LIKELY / GUESSING. The desk's epistemic discipline, extended to the room. Changes the tone of a comment section overnight.
5. **The Steelman Gate** — to post a rebuttal, the reply box first asks you to restate the point you're disagreeing with. One field, one sentence, shown above your reply. Civility by interface, not by moderator.
6. **Your Ledger (reader edition)** — readers can make calls on live stories ("Will this matter in 30 days?" "Does the bill pass?"), tagged with confidence, scored by time, building a personal **Judgment Score**. The desk keeps score on itself; now readers can too. This is also the on-ramp to the education business — practice judgment before you buy the course.
7. **The Bottom of the News** — when you've read the day's high-gravity essentials, the site *tells you you're done*: "You're caught up. The board will hold the seat. Go live your life." A news site engineered to be finishable. For a 21-year-old founder, "the news site that wants you to leave" is a national-press-worthy position against doomscrolling.
8. **The Depth Dial** — every top story readable at four depths: 10 seconds (one line) → 1 minute (the card) → 5 minutes (the dossier) → the full record (every source, every frame, the timeline). You choose your depth; the interface respects your time at every setting.
9. **Vertical share cards** — every story and every Ledger call auto-exports a 9:16 card (story, gravity score, framing spread, brand) sized for TikTok/IG/Shorts. The distribution engine for an audience the legacy desks can't reach.

### Trust & independence, instrumented (not claimed)

10. **The Tilt Meter** — a live, public dashboard of the board's own balance: source mix left/center/right this week, framing spread, story selection by beat. If the board drifts, the meter says so before a critic does. No news organization on earth publishes its own bias telemetry in real time.
11. **The Glass Desk** — open-books metrics page: subscriber count, revenue, costs, error rate from the Ledger, uptime from the sweep log. Updated monthly, in public. "Show the work" applied to the company itself.
12. **The framing spectrum strip** — a small horizontal bar on every story card showing where its coverage clusters politically (dots along L–C–R). Independence proven per-story, at a glance.
13. **Tune-it-yourself algorithm page** — on the GRAVITY explainer, readers get the actual sliders (corroboration, velocity, consequence…) and can re-weight the live board in their own browser to see what changes. Radical transparency as a toy; a civics lesson disguised as a feature.

### The 21 Story (founder-forward brand)

14. **The manifesto page** — "Founded at 21. No legacy to defend. No access to protect. Nothing to trade." The Churchill line and the second-row thesis, told as a founder story with photography and the real voice.
15. **Founder's note slot** — a short, signed note atop each day's briefing. Voice is the moat for audiences who follow people, not mastheads.
16. **The Founding 500** — the founding-member roll as a designed wall (name + member number + date), capped, with progress shown. Scarcity with receipts.
17. **Press kit page** — the 21-year-old independent newsroom is a story journalists will write; make their job effortless: bio, photos, logos, the numbers from the Glass Desk.

# PART D · GRAVITY — THE ALGORITHM, EXPLAINABLE OUT LOUD

**The sentence:** *"Five measurable signals — how many independent newsrooms, how fast it's spreading, how many people it touches, whether power is being exercised, and how fresh it is — combine into one score. High gravity rises. Low gravity stays in the Lobby. The math is public."*

| Signal | Weight | Plain meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **Corroboration** | 25 | Independent *owners* carrying it (40 sites, one parent = one) |
| **Velocity** | 20 | Acceleration of coverage, last 45 minutes |
| **Consequence** | 25 | How many people, how directly, for how long (triage-assisted, rubric public) |
| **Power** | 15 | Is power being used, abused, or checked — the civic filter |
| **Freshness** | 15 | Half-life decay; ink still cools |

- **≥ 70 = the Board** (FLASH/BULLETIN/URGENT tiers as today) · **40–69 = Developing** · **< 40 = The Lobby** (casual news, honestly labeled, never pretending to be the front page).
- Hysteresis + bump budget carry over (no flicker, no chaos).
- **Versioned & changelogged** at `/gravity`, with the reader-side sliders. When you explain it on a podcast, the host can open the page and check you.

# PART G · THE PERMANENT RECORD — secure & constant (v2.1)

**The founder's rule:** log on any day, any device, and find every comment you ever made and every story you ever saved. Nothing the reader touched is ever lost. Here is what that means, engineered:

1. **A real system of record.** Postgres (Neon) becomes the permanent home of everything human: accounts, comments, saves, reader-Ledger calls, columns, and archived story dossiers. Redis keeps only the live board (the hot 60-second state). Cache can reset; the record cannot.
2. **Nothing ever 404s.** Stories age off the live board, but every dossier a reader ever saw, saved, or commented on is archived permanently with its full biography. A comment you made in March still resolves to a living page in November. We call the whole layer **The Permanent Record**.
3. **Your Seat** (`/you`) — the account home: every comment (with links back to where it lives), your **Clippings** (saved stories), your Ledger calls and Judgment Score, your streaks, your preferences (theme, depth, sounds) synced across devices.
4. **Passwordless by design.** Magic-link sign-in, with **passkeys** (Face ID / fingerprint) as the upgrade path. No passwords exist, so no passwords can leak. Sessions are HttpOnly, SameSite, rotated; sign-ins are rate-limited.
5. **Comment safety.** All reader content is sanitized before render (no scripts, no embeds), rate-limited, honeypotted against bots, with new accounts soft-held until first approval. Every moderation action is logged. One rule for everyone.
6. **Data dignity.** Two buttons on Your Seat, no support ticket needed: **Download everything** (JSON export of all your data) and **Delete me** (full erasure, comments tombstoned). No ads, no trackers, nothing sold — already policy; now it's interface.
7. **Backups the founder owns.** Automated daily database backups, plus a monthly export to founder-owned storage. The community and the archive are assets of The Second Row, not hostages of a vendor.
8. **Constancy, monitored.** The sweep dead-man switch, error monitoring, and uptime checks page the desk before readers notice. The public sweep log remains the site's own accountability ledger.

# PART H · THE FINAL ROUND — never-heard-before, batch two

1. **The Third Act** — news sites never tell you how the story ended; this one does. Every story eventually gets a closing state — RESOLVED, FADED, or ONGOING — and an automated follow-up desk resurfaces stories 30/90 days later with what actually happened. The only feed where stories *end*.
2. **The Rewind** — scrub the front page back through time. Every sweep is already stored, so the board becomes replayable: see exactly what the Wire looked like at 9:04 p.m. on election night, forever. Historic front pages as permanent URLs.
3. **Sealed Takes** — comment as a time capsule: write your take on a developing story and *seal* it. It stays hidden until the story resolves, then all seals open at once. Receipts culture, built into the comment box.
4. **Follow the story, not the topic** — one tap follows a specific story; you're pinged only when it *develops* (tier change, major corroboration, resolution). Not another firehose — a thread that taps your shoulder when something actually happens.
5. **Your Tilt** — the Tilt Meter, pointed at yourself: a private mirror showing the framing spread of what *you've* been reading this week. Visible only to you, never shared, never sold — judgment training as a feature.
6. **The Morning Edition** — at 7 a.m. the board is frozen, typeset, numbered, and archived: a finite, collectible daily front page with a permanent URL (and it *is* the morning email). Pairs with The Bottom of the News: a day of news you can actually finish.
7. **Catch Me Up** — a button on every dossier: "I missed two weeks — what happened?" Answered in three bullets generated *only* from that story's own archived timeline and sources, receipts attached. Grounded catch-up, not vibes.
8. **The Room vs. The Desk** — on every story, the readers' aggregated certainty tags displayed beside the desk's. When the room and the desk disagree, that's shown — and that page becomes the most honest meter in news.
9. **The Second Seat** — every paying member can gift one free month to one person: "the seat next to you." Growth that travels person-to-person, the way trust actually does.
10. **The caught-up streak** — the anti-engagement metric: streaks reward *finishing* the day's essentials, not lingering. The first news site whose loyalty program is built on letting you leave.
